Antiquated myths tell us that around 1300 a long time back when the national capital was built in Nara, Takemikazuchi-no-mikoto came all the way from Kashima Holy place (Ibaraki Prefecture) to Mt. Mikasa, which is considered a heavenly mountain, to stay on its summit “Ukigumo-no-mine” for the success of the country and bliss of the individuals. Afterward on, when Tempyo Culture prospered, the political pioneer Fujiwara-no-Nagate built wonderful buildings on the show location of the holy place as appointed by Ruler Shotoku. On November 9, 768, he cherished a few divine beings here, specifically Futsunushi-no-mikoto from Katori Holy place (Chiba Prefecture), and Amenokoyane-no-mikoto and Himegami from Hiraoka Holy place (Osaka Prefecture). Usually how Kasuga Taisha started.

Mount Fuji is the tallest mountain in Japan and is known for its elegant cone-shaped shape.
It is the country’s sacrosanct image, and temples and shrines are found around and on the spring of gushing lava.
Climbing the mountain has long been a devout hone, and Fuji is one of Japan’s most prevalent traveler attractions.

Culture in Japan

Local Festivals & Events

Miyoshi Ojochin Giant Lantern Festival

A dramatic 450-year-old festival featuring huge lanterns to keep sea monsters at bay.A stone's toss from shining Chita Inlet in Aichi Prefecture , the tremendous paper lights of the Mammoth Light Celebration, held in late Eminent, are one of a kind cases of long-standing convention and artistry.

Omihachiman Sagicho Fire Festival

The story behind the Omihachiman Sagicho Fire Celebration is vague. A few say it was begun by the warlord Oda Nobunaga within the 1500s. Others say it was to celebrate the warlord's passing and his castle burning down.

Yunishigawa Kamakura Festival

The Yunishigawa Kamakura Celebration is held each year from late January to early Walk. Hundreds of snow hovels, or kamakura, fill the roads of this little onsen town. Best known for its nighttime brightening of the cabins, get your camera and head to Yunishigawa to appreciate this winter wonderland.
