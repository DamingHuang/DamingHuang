United States of America



The Amendments

I. Protects freedom of religion, freedom of speech, freedom of the press, freedom of assembly and the right to petition the government.

II. Protects the right to keep and bear arms

III. Restricts the quartering of soldiers in private homes

IV. Prohibits unreasonable searches and seizures and sets out requirements for search warrants based on probable cause

V. Sets out rules for indictment by grand jury and eminent domain, protects the right to due process, and prohibits self-incrimination and double jeopardy

VI. Protects the right to a speedy public trial by jury, to notification of criminal accusations, to confront the accuser, to obtain witnesses and to retain counsel

VII. Provides for the right to a jury trial in civil lawsuits

VIII. Prohibits excessive fines and excessive bail, as well as cruel and unusual punishment

IX. States that rights not enumerated in the Constitution are retained by the people

X. States that the federal government possesses only those powers delegated, or enumerated, to it through the Constitution

XI. Makes states immune from suits from out-of-state citizens and foreigners not living within the state borders; lays the foundation for state sovereign immunity

XII. Revises presidential election procedures by having the president and vice president elected together as opposed to the vice president being the runner up in the presidential election

XIII. Abolishes slavery, and involuntary servitude, except as punishment for a crime

XIV. Defines citizenship, contains the Privileges or Immunities Clause, the Due Process Clause, and the Equal Protection Clause, and deals with post–Civil War issues

XV. Prohibits the denial of the right to vote based on race, color or previous condition of servitude

XVI. Permits Congress to levy an income tax without apportioning it among the various states or basing it on the United States Census

XVII. Establishes the direct election of United States senators by popular vote

XVIII. Prohibited the manufacturing or sale of alcohol within the United States

XIX. (Repealed December 5, 1933, via the 21st Amendment)

XX. Prohibits the denial of the right to vote based on sex

XXI. Changes the dates on which the terms of the President and Vice President, and of members of Congress, begin and end, to January 20 and January 3 respectively. States that if the President-elect dies before taking office, the Vice President–elect is to be inaugurated as President.

XXII. Repeals the 18th Amendment and makes it a federal offense to transport or import intoxicating liquors into U.S. states and territories where such is prohibited by law

XXIII. Limits the number of times a person can be elected President. XXIV. Grants the District of Columbia electors in the Electoral College XXV. Prohibits the revocation of voting rights due to the non-payment of a poll tax or any other tax

XXVI. Addresses succession to the presidency and establishes procedures both for filling a vacancy in the office of the vice president and responding to presidential disabilities

XXVII. Prohibits the denial of the right of US citizens eighteen years of age or older to vote on account of age

XXVIII. Delays laws affecting Congressional salary from taking effect until after the next election of representatives
